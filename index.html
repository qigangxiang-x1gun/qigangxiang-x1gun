Objective

I aim to develop reliable robotic systems that combine perception, planning and manipulation for practical autonomous tasks. My current focus is ROS 2-based mobile robotics, RGB-D perception, object detection, and robotic manipulation for service robotics, healthcare and automation.

Focused Research Interests

My research interests are focused on robot perception and mobile manipulation, including RGB-D sensing, visual object detection, 2D-to-3D target localisation, grasping, SLAM-based navigation and requirement-based robot testing.

Autonomous Rover for Object Retrieval and Sorting

Goal: Design and evaluate a ROS 2-based mobile manipulator that explores an indoor arena, detects coloured blocks, retrieves them and sorts them into matching bins.
My contribution: Supported manipulation and verification work, including grasp strategy, lift requirement analysis, test planning, requirement evidence and integration debugging.
Skills: ROS 2, Nav2, SLAM Toolbox, RealSense, RPLidar, YOLOv8, myCobot, requirement-based testing.

Continuous Professional Development

My CPD reflection is based on UK-SPEC competencies and the engineering skills developed through the robotic systems design project. My current development focus is practical system testing, continuous improvement, and safe robot operation.

B3 – Implement and Evaluate Engineering Solutions

I aim to improve my ability to turn robotic designs into reliable working systems through repeatable testing, quantitative evaluation and failure analysis. Future activities include ROS 2 testing, sensor calibration, manipulation tuning and structured hardware trials.

C4 – Continuous Improvement and Best Practice

I plan to improve my engineering practice by using test logs, lessons learned, version control, design reviews and evidence-based updates after each test cycle.

E2 – Safety and Safe Systems of Work

I want to strengthen my ability to identify hazards and define safe procedures for autonomous robots, including pre-test checklists, emergency stop validation and safe manipulator operation.
