Objective

My academic and technical objective is to develop reliable robotic systems that integrate perception, planning and manipulation for real-world autonomous tasks. I am particularly interested in ROS 2-based mobile robotics, RGB-D perception, object detection and robotic manipulation for service robotics, healthcare and automation.

Focused Research Interests

My research interests focus on robot perception and mobile manipulation, especially RGB-D sensing, visual object detection, 2D-to-3D target localisation, grasp execution, SLAM-based navigation and requirement-based robotic system evaluation.

Research Direction

My research direction is intelligent robotics, especially:
- Robot perception using RGB-D cameras and vision models
- Autonomous navigation and mapping using ROS 2, SLAM and Nav2
- Robotic manipulation and grasping for small object handling
- Safe and measurable robot testing in real environments

Selected Projects and Research Experience

The projects below highlight my experience in robotic system design, computer vision, machine learning, prototyping and requirement-based evaluation. Each project includes its goal, my contribution, technical skills and supporting evidence.

Autonomous Rover for Object Retrieval and Sorting

Goal: Design and evaluate a ROS 2-based mobile manipulator for autonomous indoor exploration, coloured-object detection, object retrieval and bin sorting.
My contribution: Supported manipulation and verification work, including grasp strategy, payload analysis, test planning, requirement evidence and integration debugging.
Skills: ROS 2, Nav2, SLAM Toolbox, RealSense, RPLidar, YOLOv8, myCobot, requirement-based testing.

My contribution: Participated in robot design discussions, supported visual-recognition work, prepared milestone reports and contributed to project documentation.
